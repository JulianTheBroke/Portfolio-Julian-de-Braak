Cook A Montage (CAM)

Lead Programmer · Unity · Team of 4

A cooking-show game where you sit behind the screens in the camera booth, not at the stove.

Sandra cooks on set. You switch cameras, follow the action, and keep the right shots recording. Producer notes tell you what matters. Quick-time events and other scoring rules decide how well you covered each step. When the recipe is done, you get a score and watch your montage replay to see what you captured.

CAM = Cook A Montage.

My Contribution

What I Did

Programming & Integration

  • Lead programmer on a shared codebase, wired menus, kitchen, camera booth, and recipe flow together
  • Fixed builds and scene issues so we always had something runnable to test and present
  • Managed version control on the team repo
  • Ran playtests and fixed blockers before demo

Shipping Under Pressure

  • Built an early camera-switching prototype in a few days so the team had something concrete to react to. Not a full game, but proof the director fantasy could work
  • We had scoped the full game loop (direct, record, QTE, score, replay) but not every piece was ready at once; art, UI, and systems landed on different schedules
  • Close to deadline I pulled what we had into one build and wired the gaps so we could demo that loop as close as possible to what we designed: one recipe, start to finish, with a score and replay at the end

Outside Code (Limited)

  • Made a few early Blender placeholders to unblock the team; most were replaced and did not ship
  • One asset I textured in Substance Painter did make it into the final game

How I Work

  • Define the problem: what the feature or system needs to do in the game
  • Research: look at references and how similar problems are already solved
  • Build a basic version myself, getting something running even if it's rough
  • Debug and iterate: fix what breaks, and tighten it until it holds together in a build

I also use AI as a tool in that same loop for faster lookup, drafting, or debugging, but never for creative decisions. The design and what ships stay mine.

Core Loop

How a Recipe Plays

  1. Read the producer notes for what to film next
  2. Switch cameras and follow Sandra; keep key moments recording
  3. Hit QTEs and meet scoring criteria as she cooks
  4. Get your score breakdown for the recipe
  5. Watch your montage replay

Relevance

What This Demonstrates

Client-Style Production. Integration across scenes, GitHub, playtests, and a hard deadline. Closer to how a studio ships a vertical slice than a solo art piece.
Lead Programmer Ownership. Wired menus, kitchen, camera booth, and recipe flow into one runnable loop the team could demo.
Honest Process. Documented what I'd improve, including scope calls and knowing when to cut rather than oversell.
Reflection

What I'd Improve

Lock in the core loop earlier, before adding recipe scope. We knew the full loop early but kept building outward before the director flow felt solid end to end.
Own critical systems by hand from start to finish. I used AI to move faster, but I want a deeper understanding of every system that ships in a build I present.
A clearer split between programming lead and integration. On a four-person team I ended up doing both, and earlier boundaries would have reduced last-minute firefighting.
