How I Got Here

Background

I didn't come into game development through a straight path. I started at Hanze in ICT on the NSE track, so networking, security, and infrastructure. Computers were always my thing, but I couldn't picture spending the rest of my career in a server room.

Creative Media and Game Technologies gave that restlessness a direction I actually wanted to follow. Before that I'd drifted between music, video editing, 3D, pixel art, and small game experiments, always learning enough to put something on screen but never staying with one thing long enough to feel like I'd really got good at it. Working in teams is mostly what changed that habit, because when other people are waiting on your work and there's a date on the calendar you can't hide behind "I'll figure it out later." You learn quickly or the demo falls apart, and that's how I went from touching a lot of tools to actually trying to finish games.

On a Team

What I Bring

When a project needs direction, I step up, not to hoard work but because I want the game to work on demo day.

Communication and Coordination. I keep art, design, and code aligned, delegate where it makes sense, and make sure people can play to their strengths.
Direct About Scope. I will say when something is too much or the build is not ready. I would rather cut than oversell what we have.
Integration and Builds. I wire systems together, fix merges, and keep a runnable build in the repo. On CAM that meant menus, kitchen, camera booth, and recipe flow in one loop.
Technical Support. If someone is stuck on a programming problem, I help them through it, and if time is short I take it on so the team can keep moving.
Runnable Demos. Playtests, blockers, and last-mile fixes so the group always has something to show.
Beyond Code When It Helps. I composed music on C.R.I.T.T.E.R. and edit video in Premiere Pro for clips and portfolio material. On CAM I modeled a few early 3D placeholders myself, because I was already working in that part of the project and needed assets faster than waiting on a handoff.

Direction

Where I Am Headed

I'm aiming at junior technical production, still hands-on but involved in more than one part of how a project comes together. I don't want to be boxed in as "just the programmer" in the corner; I want a real say in how a prototype gets built and what ships.

Next year I have a VR team project in Unity and a separate Unreal Engine project at school. GameMaker was a solid one-off through C.R.I.T.T.E.R., but long-term I'm centred on Unity and Unreal. I'm planning a personal side project too, still deciding what, and Godot is something I might use for that down the line. I'm building toward an internship in year three, using work like this to be ready when that opens up.
